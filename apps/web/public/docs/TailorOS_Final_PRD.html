Final production PRD

TailorOS

TailorOS is a mobile-first operating system for Tamil Nadu tailor shops. It replaces scattered notebooks, phone calls, and manual receipts with searchable family-aware customer records, measurement history, order tracking, production workflow, payments, receipts, reports, and event hooks for a separate WhatsApp Chat Connector.

Prepared July 3, 2026
  • Recommended v1: mobile-responsive web app, not native app.
  • Core rule: mobile number is a contact channel, not the customer identity.
  • Every family, customer, measurement, order, item, payment, and receipt gets a stable ID.
  • WhatsApp is integrated through a separate reusable connector, not hardcoded into TailorOS.

Product decision

Build a shop operating system first, then automate communication

Production-grade recommendation

TailorOS should launch as an operational tool for the shop counter and owner, not as a customer portal. The first win is faster order entry, fewer measurement mistakes, fewer missed delivery dates, clearer balances, and reduced customer status calls. WhatsApp should amplify those workflows only after TailorOS has clean customer, order, payment, and status data.

Primary buyer

Owner of a tailor shop, boutique, uniform shop, or alteration-focused store that manages many repeat customers and family orders.

Primary daily users

Owner, manager, counter staff, measurement taker, tailor, production supervisor, and platform support admin.

Primary business result

A shop can confidently answer: who is the customer, what was ordered, what measurements were used, where is the garment, what is due, and what was paid.

Scope boundary

What TailorOS v1 includes and excludes

In scope for v1

  • Tenant/shop setup, branch setup, staff roles, and configurable ID prefixes.
  • Family-aware customer directory where one mobile number can have multiple customer profiles.
  • Universal search by mobile, name, family ID, customer ID, order ID, receipt ID, and status.
  • Garment-specific measurement templates, version history, photos, fit notes, and order-specific overrides.
  • New order wizard with multiple order items, trial date, promised date, price, advance, balance, and receipt.
  • Production workflow with item-level statuses, tailor assignment, exception reasons, and daily work board.
  • Payments, receipts, balance tracking, payment correction audit trail, and owner reports.
  • WhatsApp-ready event hooks, notification logs, opt-in capture, and inbound status lookup support.

Out of scope for v1

  • Native Android or iOS app. Use mobile-responsive web first.
  • Full GST accounting, tax filing, purchase ledger, and financial ERP.
  • Advanced inventory for fabric rolls, supplier purchase orders, and stock valuation.
  • Payroll, tailor commission automation, and HR attendance.
  • Public ecommerce website or customer self-service portal.
  • AI fashion design recommendations or automated measurement capture.
  • Bulk marketing campaigns. Start with utility and service communication only.
  • Offline-first full app. Add offline draft capture after the pilot proves core usage.

Product principles

Rules that should guide every screen

Search first

The first action is almost always search. The app should not force staff to choose a module before finding the customer or order.

Mobile is not identity

A phone number may represent a whole family. Measurements and orders belong to individual customer profiles.

Order is the record

Receipts, payments, statuses, tasks, notifications, and disputes must trace back to a stable order ID and item IDs.

Measurements are historical

Past orders must keep the measurement version used at that time, even if the customer's current measurement changes.

Promises need dates

Every order item needs a promised date or a visible reason why the date is missing.

WhatsApp is controlled

Customers should receive useful updates, not every internal status change. Opt-in, opt-out, and message history matter.

Core flows

End-to-end workflows to support

New walk-in order

  1. Counter staff searches mobile number first.
  2. If no family exists, staff creates a family account and then the exact customer profile.
  3. Staff selects garment type, captures or copies measurements, and adds fit notes.
  4. Staff creates the order, adds items, dates, price, advance, and balance.
  5. TailorOS generates order ID, receipt ID, and optional WhatsApp confirmation event.

Repeat customer with family mobile

  1. Staff searches the shared mobile number.
  2. TailorOS shows grouped profiles under the family account.
  3. Staff selects the right person before viewing measurements.
  4. Staff copies latest garment-specific measurement and adds item override if needed.
  5. Receipt clearly shows customer name, order ID, and balance due.

Production and trial

  1. Owner or manager assigns item tasks to tailors.
  2. Tailor moves work through material confirmed, cutting, stitching, finishing, and trial.
  3. If alteration is needed, staff adds alteration log and updated trial note.
  4. Delays require a reason such as customer delay, material shortage, or on hold.
  5. When all items are ready, TailorOS emits ready-for-pickup event.

Pickup and payment close

  1. Staff opens order by order ID, customer search, or ready-for-pickup dashboard.
  2. Staff confirms item completion and collects remaining balance.
  3. Payment is recorded with mode and reference.
  4. Receipt updates to paid or partial status.
  5. Order is marked delivered and closed after final handover.

Milestone plan

Deep production milestone analysis

Each milestone must leave the app usable in the browser and testable by a shop owner. Later milestones build on data quality created earlier.

M1

Shop foundation, roles, and ID system

Create the control base for a multi-client TailorOS installation: shop setup, branch setup, staff roles, and stable IDs.

Build scope

  • Tenant/shop profile with branding and default language.
  • Branch records and shop code prefixes.
  • Owner, manager, front desk, tailor, and platform admin roles.
  • ID format rules for family, customer, order, item, receipt, payment, and measurement.

Deep analysis

  • This milestone prevents future migration pain. If IDs and roles are weak, every later module becomes fragile.
  • Use simple defaults but make prefixes configurable before pilot.
  • Support access must be audited because platform staff may see client data.

Done when

  • A platform admin can create a shop and branch.
  • An owner can invite staff and assign roles.
  • New records generate readable, stable IDs with the shop prefix.
  • Restricted users cannot access payment corrections or settings.
M2

Family-aware customer directory and universal search

Make customer lookup fast enough for busy counter work while preventing family-member measurement mixups.

Build scope

  • Family account creation with primary and alternate mobile numbers.
  • Multiple customer profiles under one family account.
  • Search by mobile, name, family ID, customer ID, and alternate phone.
  • Duplicate warning when the same number or similar name exists.

Deep analysis

  • This is the adoption milestone. If staff cannot find a person quickly, they return to notebooks.
  • Search results should group family members and highlight active orders later.
  • Mobile number normalization is required so +91, leading zero, and spacing do not create duplicates.

Done when

  • One mobile number can show several family members.
  • Staff must choose the exact profile before measurements appear.
  • Customer details can be edited with audit history.
  • Search works from the first screen.
M3

Measurement center with versioning and overrides

Capture garment-specific measurements in a way that protects historical orders and supports real tailoring exceptions.

Build scope

  • Measurement templates for blouse, shirt, pant, salwar or kurta, uniform, and alteration.
  • Measurement profile per customer and garment type.
  • Version history with reason, changed by, and changed date.
  • Photos, fit preferences, unit handling, warning notes, and current/old flags.

Deep analysis

  • Measurements are the highest trust area. Never overwrite without history.
  • Units must be visible because inch/cm confusion creates costly errors.
  • Order-specific overrides belong to order items, not the permanent customer profile.

Done when

  • Staff can create and update measurements by garment type.
  • Old versions remain visible.
  • Staff can attach photos and warning notes.
  • A changed measurement never breaks an older order's snapshot.
M4

New order wizard and order book

Create the daily revenue flow: repeat customer selection, garment items, measurement snapshots, dates, pricing, and receipt draft.

Build scope

  • Order wizard from search result or customer profile.
  • Multiple garments per order with item IDs.
  • Measurement snapshot and item-level override fields.
  • Trial date, promised date, price, discount, advance, balance, and notes.

Deep analysis

  • Order entry should target under 5 minutes for new customers and around 2 minutes for repeat customers.
  • The wizard should default common garment data but never hide the selected customer identity.
  • Items need independent statuses because one garment may be ready while another is delayed.

Done when

  • Staff can create an order from an existing or new customer.
  • Order and item IDs are generated.
  • The order stores measurement version used and any override.
  • The order summary is printable or ready for receipt generation.
M5

Production board, task assignment, and alteration logs

Give owners and tailors a shared view of what is due, what is stuck, and who owns each item.

Build scope

  • Status lanes for material confirmed, cutting, stitching, finishing, trial, alteration, ready, delivered, closed.
  • Exception statuses: on hold, customer delay, material shortage, cancelled, refunded.
  • Tailor assignment and task comments.
  • Alteration log with issue, requested change, trial result, and chargeable flag.

Deep analysis

  • This milestone makes TailorOS useful beyond the counter.
  • Status design should not spam customers; only selected statuses become message events.
  • Delay reasons are required for owner visibility and future reporting.

Done when

  • Owner can view work by status, tailor, and due date.
  • Tailor can update assigned item status without seeing financial reports.
  • Ready orders appear in a pickup list.
  • Alteration history is visible on order item timeline.
M6

Payments, receipts, and balance control

Make money records clear enough for the owner while keeping receipts simple for local shop use.

Build scope

  • Advance and balance payment capture.
  • Payment modes: cash, UPI, card, bank, credit, and adjustment.
  • Receipt page with item summary, paid amount, balance, and shop branding.
  • Correction workflow with audit trail.

Deep analysis

  • Payments are dispute-sensitive. Avoid silent edits.
  • Simple receipts are enough for v1; GST mode can be a later configuration.
  • Receipts must link to order ID and customer profile, not only phone number.

Done when

  • Staff can record advance and final payment.
  • Owner can see pending balances and today's collection.
  • Receipt can be printed or shared as a link/PDF.
  • Payment corrections show who changed what and why.
M7

Dashboards, reports, and operating visibility

Turn TailorOS from a record system into a daily operating cockpit.

Build scope

  • Today dashboard for due, overdue, ready, trial, and payment due.
  • Production board summary by tailor and status.
  • Customer history and repeat order report.
  • Money report for collection, pending balance, refunds, and corrections.

Deep analysis

  • Reports should answer what to do next, not only show totals.
  • Owners need scanning views more than decorative charts.
  • Every report row should open the related order, customer, or task.

Done when

  • Owner can open TailorOS and know today's work in under 30 seconds.
  • Pending balances are visible by order and customer.
  • Delayed orders show reason and owner action.
  • Reports support export only for authorized roles.
M8

WhatsApp readiness and notification log

Prepare TailorOS to work with the separate WhatsApp Chat Connector without embedding provider logic inside TailorOS.

Build scope

  • Consent capture on customer contact methods.
  • Event emission for orders, receipts, payments, appointments, pickup, and opt-out.
  • Notification log on customer and order pages.
  • Customer status lookup rules for shared mobile numbers.

Deep analysis

  • TailorOS should know business events; the connector should know WhatsApp rules.
  • Shared phone numbers require disambiguation before revealing order details.
  • Failed sends need staff-visible fallback actions.

Done when

  • Staff can see opt-in and opt-out state before sending.
  • Events are visible in a local notification log even before provider connection.
  • Incoming status requests can map to active orders or ask for customer/order choice.
  • TailorOS does not store provider access tokens.
M9

Pilot onboarding, imports, privacy, and audit hardening

Make the product safe and usable for the first real shop pilot.

Build scope

  • Shop onboarding checklist and initial settings review.
  • Spreadsheet import for historical customers and active orders.
  • Privacy controls for measurement photos, exports, and support access.
  • Consent wording, data retention policy fields, and audit review screen.

Deep analysis

  • The first shop should start with 50 historical customers and 20 active orders.
  • Run parallel with the notebook for two weeks and compare accuracy.
  • Support access must be permissioned and logged before scaling to many clients.

Done when

  • A pilot shop can be onboarded with real data.
  • Owner can review privacy, consent, and export controls.
  • Staff training checklist exists inside the admin flow.
  • Pilot metrics can be captured weekly.
M10

v1 launch hardening and v1.5 planning

Stabilize for 3 to 5 shop rollout and prepare the next useful additions without bloating v1.

Build scope

  • Error states, empty states, export permissions, backup review, and support workflows.
  • Owner-facing success dashboard for pilot metrics.
  • Configuration review for garment templates, statuses, and receipt layout.
  • v1.5 plan for simple inventory ledger and offline draft capture.

Deep analysis

  • Do not add every requested feature before the pilot. First stabilize order, measurement, payment, and production flows.
  • Simple stock ledger is useful after order volume is proven.
  • Offline drafts are valuable, but risky if conflict handling is not designed carefully.

Done when

  • First shop can use TailorOS daily without notebook dependency for active orders.
  • Known pilot issues are tracked and prioritized.
  • Onboarding package is ready for 3 to 5 more shops.
  • v1.5 scope is documented but not mixed into v1 acceptance.

Critical risks

  • Order entry takes too long and staff return to notebooks.
  • Phone number is treated as the customer identity and family records mix up.
  • Measurements are overwritten without historical versions.
  • Status workflow becomes too complex for small shops.
  • Payments are edited without audit and owner trust drops.
  • WhatsApp messages are overused and customers opt out.

Launch controls

  • Use default garment templates but allow shop-level edits.
  • Require reason for payment corrections, measurement changes, and delay exceptions.
  • Keep marketing out of v1 WhatsApp workflows.
  • Make export, delete, and support access owner/platform-admin only.
  • Run one-shop pilot before onboarding multiple shops.
  • Document local Tamil consent wording with owner review before production use.
