Product requirements document

What we are building

The product is a simple but complete tailor shop operating system for Tamil Nadu based shops. It replaces notebooks, memory, scattered WhatsApp chats, and manual calls with a structured order book, measurement history, receipt trail, and automated WhatsApp status updates.

The first release focuses on daily tailor shop work: create an order quickly, attach measurements and garment details, track work status, collect advance and balance payments, send receipts, and let customers check their order status through WhatsApp.

Primary users

  • Shop owner: wants visibility over pending work, income, delivery promises, and customer satisfaction.
  • Front desk staff: creates orders, collects payments, updates customers, and prints or sends receipts.
  • Tailor or master: needs a clear work queue, fitting notes, garment details, and delivery priority.
  • Customer: wants order status, due date, receipt, balance, fitting reminder, and pickup alert on WhatsApp.
  • Platform admin: manages client shops, WhatsApp accounts, usage, templates, and subscription plans.

Target shop types

  • Local men's and women's tailoring shops.
  • Boutiques handling blouse, salwar, kurta, shirt, pant, uniform, wedding, and alteration orders.
  • Small shops with 1 to 5 staff where WhatsApp is already the main customer channel.
  • Growing shops that want repeat-customer records and fewer manual status calls.

Core in-scope features

Customer profiles

Store name, mobile number, alternate number, address, notes, consent for WhatsApp updates, and past order history.

Measurement history

Save measurement sets by garment type, customer, date, and version so repeat orders do not require re-measuring every time.

Order creation

Capture garment type, quantity, fabric photo, design notes, promised date, fitting date, price, advance, balance, and priority.

Status tracking

Use clear stages such as new, measured, cutting, stitching, fitting, alteration, ready, delivered, cancelled, and delayed.

Receipts and balances

Generate a clean receipt with order number, items, advance, balance, due date, and shop terms. Send it over WhatsApp.

WhatsApp notifications

Send order confirmation, fitting reminders, ready alerts, delay notices, receipts, and balance reminders with approved templates.

Customer status chat

Customers can message the shop number and choose status, receipt, balance, fitting date, pickup time, or human support.

Reports

Show due today, overdue orders, pending balances, delivered orders, repeat customers, and monthly revenue.

Multi-client admin

Platform owner can onboard shops, connect WhatsApp credentials, manage usage, and see integration health per client.

Out of scope for version 1

Do not build first

  • Native Android or iOS app. Start with responsive web.
  • Full accounting, GST filing, or inventory management.
  • Advanced payroll or tailor salary calculation.
  • Online ecommerce storefront or catalog checkout.
  • Heavy AI styling assistant or design recommendation engine.

Delay until traction

  • Marketing broadcast campaigns beyond opt-in customers.
  • Multi-branch enterprise controls.
  • Advanced barcode scanning and label printing.
  • Deep payment gateway integration.
  • Complex custom workflow builder for every shop.

Core workflows

How the product should feel in daily use

New order flow

  1. Staff enters customer mobile number and selects an existing customer or creates a new one.
  2. Staff chooses garment type, copies existing measurements or captures new measurements.
  3. Staff adds promised date, fitting date, price, advance, balance, notes, and fabric or design photo.
  4. System generates an order number and receipt.
  5. Customer receives WhatsApp confirmation with order number, delivery date, amount paid, and balance.

Customer status flow

  1. Customer sends a message to the shop's WhatsApp number.
  2. System identifies the customer from the mobile number.
  3. If there are multiple active orders, customer chooses the order number.
  4. System replies with status, expected date, balance, receipt link, and next action.
  5. Customer can request human support if the answer is not enough.

Staff status update flow

  • Staff moves an order from one status to another.
  • The app asks whether to notify the customer when the status is customer-facing.
  • Customer receives only useful updates, such as fitting reminder, ready for pickup, delay, or delivered.
  • The status timeline remains visible inside the order page.

Receipt and balance flow

  • Every payment creates a receipt event.
  • Staff can send the latest receipt on WhatsApp in one click.
  • Customer can later ask for receipt or balance through chat.
  • The system never exposes other customers' data when a phone number is shared or changed.

Plain answer: which is most cost-efficient and easy?

Most cost-efficient long term: Meta Cloud API direct. Easiest to start quickly: Twilio or an India-focused BSP dashboard. Best fit for your multi-product, multi-client goal: Meta Cloud API direct with a provider-adapter architecture, keeping Twilio as optional.

Message strategy

Use WhatsApp for trust, not noise

Message categories to use

  • Service: customer messages the shop first, then the shop replies within the 24-hour service window. This is best for order-status chat.
  • Utility: transactional updates such as order confirmation, receipt, fitting reminder, ready for pickup, delay update, and balance reminder.
  • Authentication: not needed in v1 unless customers log into a portal with OTP.
  • Marketing: keep out of v1 except opt-in festival campaigns later. It is more expensive and more sensitive for spam complaints.

Recommended templates

  • order_confirmation
  • payment_receipt
  • measurement_reminder
  • fitting_reminder
  • order_ready_pickup
  • delay_update
  • balance_due
  • alteration_ready
  • delivery_confirmation

Customer chat menu example

Welcome to [Shop Name]. Choose an option: 1. Check order status, 2. View receipt, 3. Check balance, 4. Fitting or pickup date, 5. Talk to staff.

For Tamil Nadu rollout, prepare templates in English and Tamil. Keep the first release bilingual, but avoid complex AI translation until the operational flow is stable.

Separate WhatsApp product

Multi-client WhatsApp Messaging Hub

This should be its own product layer. The tailor app should not talk directly to Meta, Twilio, or any BSP. It should call your Messaging Hub, and the hub decides which provider, credential, template, and billing bucket applies for that client.

Provider adapters

Meta Cloud API, Twilio, and optional BSP adapters expose one normalized behavior to your apps.

Tenant isolation

Each client has its own WhatsApp account, credentials, templates, message logs, usage, and billing.

Central webhooks

Inbound messages, delivery receipts, failures, and reads come into one hub and are routed to the correct client app.

Template registry

Store template names, languages, categories, provider IDs, approval status, and variable rules per client.

Usage metering

Track sent, delivered, failed, service, utility, authentication, and marketing messages per client and period.

Integration API

Other products can request messages, status lookups, and webhook subscriptions without knowing provider-specific details.

Hub capabilities in scope

Client onboarding

  • Create client tenant and product access.
  • Add WhatsApp provider and credentials.
  • Test connection and webhook health.
  • Register or sync templates.
  • Set default language and sending windows.

Messaging operations

  • Send approved template messages.
  • Send free-form replies inside service windows.
  • Receive inbound messages and route to the owning product.
  • Track sent, delivered, read, failed, and cost category.
  • Pause sending for a client if billing or credentials are unhealthy.

Security controls

  • Encrypt client credentials.
  • Restrict provider credentials to the owning tenant.
  • Record admin changes in an audit log.
  • Rotate keys and revoke access cleanly.
  • Verify incoming provider webhooks.

Billing controls

  • Estimate charge by message category and provider.
  • Support prepaid wallet, monthly invoice, or pass-through billing.
  • Set client-level credit limits.
  • Expose monthly usage reports to client admins.
  • Flag high marketing usage before costs surprise the shop.

Credential model

Each client should bring or authorize their own WhatsApp Business Account and phone number. The platform stores only the credentials needed to send and receive messages for that client. Never share one business number across unrelated shops, because customers will lose trust and data isolation becomes risky.

  • Send only status changes customers actually care about.
  • Use customer-initiated service windows for status checks.
  • Keep marketing messages out of the default workflow.
  • Deduplicate repeated updates within the same day.
  • Show admins the estimated cost before bulk or scheduled sends.

Scale-up suggestions

How to make the product succeed

Product positioning

  • Sell the result: fewer customer calls, fewer missed promises, faster pickup, and better repeat orders.
  • Do not sell it as generic CRM. Tailor shops need order clarity, measurement memory, and delivery discipline.
  • Make the first screen a daily work board: due today, fitting today, ready for pickup, pending balance.
  • Keep order creation under two minutes, otherwise staff will return to notebooks.

Tamil Nadu market fit

  • Support Tamil and English customer templates.
  • Include blouse, salwar, kurta, shirt, pant, uniform, wedding, and alteration workflows.
  • Support festival-season capacity pressure: Deepavali, Pongal, wedding season, and school uniform periods.
  • Make WhatsApp consent simple: printed receipt note, order form checkbox, and in-shop QR code.

Go-to-market

  • Start with 3 pilot shops in one city or district before spreading wider.
  • Offer setup help: import customers, create measurement templates, connect WhatsApp, train staff.
  • Use a 7-day live demo with real orders, not a brochure.
  • Build referral incentives for boutique owners and tailoring material suppliers.

Defensibility

  • Your data moat is measurement history and customer repeat-order patterns.
  • Your workflow moat is tailoring-specific order status and reminder logic.
  • Your integration moat is the reusable WhatsApp hub serving multiple products and clients.
  • Your service moat is local onboarding, Tamil templates, and practical support.

Risks and mitigations

What can go wrong

Risk Why it matters Mitigation
Staff do not enter orders consistently The app fails if the order book is incomplete. Make order entry extremely fast, support repeat measurements, and keep the daily board valuable.
WhatsApp template rejection Key notifications may not go live on time. Write clear transactional templates, avoid promotional language, and submit templates early.
Customer spam complaints WhatsApp number quality can degrade. Use explicit opt-in, avoid marketing in v1, provide stop/opt-out handling, and send only useful updates.
Client credential confusion Multi-client data or billing can become messy. Use tenant isolation, encrypted credentials, audit logs, and clear onboarding checklists.
Provider pricing changes Margins can change suddenly. Keep provider adapters, pass message charges transparently, and update rate cards monthly.
Phone number migration issues Existing WhatsApp numbers may need account deletion or migration steps. Document number requirements and offer onboarding support before promising go-live date.
